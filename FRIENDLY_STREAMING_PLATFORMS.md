Twitch (with VODs disabled): Twitch is the most popular choice for DJs. While it is a recording-based platform, you can disable the "Store past broadcasts" feature in your creator dashboard, allowing you to stream live without a permanent video archive remaining on your channel. Note that while live, your audio may still be scanned for copyright.

Mixcloud Live: Designed specifically for music, this platform is considered one of the best for legal streaming, as it has licenses that allow you to play copyrighted music without your stream being taken down. While they may create an audio recording, the live-only video aspect is much more lenient than other platforms, and they are designed not to mute or remove your sets due to copyright.

Instagram Live: Ideal for temporary, in-the-moment streaming. Your videos only stay on your story for 24 hours, making it a "disappearing" video platform, though it lacks the high-quality desktop integration of Twitch.

TikTok Live: A popular choice for high-reach, temporary streaming. TikTok allows you to stream live video from your DJ booth and does not heavily rely on storing long-form video archives like YouTube or Twitch.
